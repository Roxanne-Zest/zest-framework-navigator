# Path to Service Assessment

The front door of Path to Service. A fixed-scope engagement that baselines a client on the maturity grid, agrees the outcomes they want from the catalogue, and produces a sequenced, pack-mapped roadmap — which is also the quote for the delivery work that follows.

## Why this pack exists
The framework describes maturity and the delivery packs build it, but nothing measured where a client actually is. Without a baseline there is no gap, no roadmap, and no defensible ROI (benefits need a "before"). This tool closes all three at once.

## What it produces
1. Scored grid — every class scored 0–3 per band, evidence-graded
2. Findings log — what was seen, where, and what it blocks
3. Outcome selection — 2–4 outcomes from the catalogue, agreed with the client
4. Sequenced roadmap — the element and outcome packs required, dependency-ordered, effort-mapped
5. Benefit baseline sheet — current values for each chosen outcome's benefit metric, captured now so later ROI is evidence, not assertion

## How it fits the kit
- Demand: outcome selection runs off the 35-outcome catalogue
- Supply: the roadmap is expressed in pack ids; effort comes from pack commercials
- State: baseline and benefit readings seed the engagement record (Slice C, later)
- Trust: same ladder as every pack; build.md is gate:test because its instance-inspection steps name specific Freshservice screens and endpoints that must be verified live

⚠️ trust:draft — do not run on a paying client until reviewed, and until build.md's inspection steps are verified in a sandbox.
