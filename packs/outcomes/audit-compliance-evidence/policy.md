### The rules
- **Target standard(s)** are agreed with the client up front and written here — they
  determine the required element packs and the evidence set.
- **The CMDB is the authoritative evidence source.** Side spreadsheets are not evidence; if
  something is being tracked outside the CMDB, that's a gap to close, not a parallel record.
- **Scope alignment** — the CMDB's in-scope flag *is* the audit scope. They cannot diverge.
- **Cadence & retention** — evidence is refreshed on a defined cadence and retained per the
  standard's requirement (point-in-time snapshots kept for the audit trail).
- **Sign-off** — the client's compliance / security owner confirms the evidence set satisfies
  the standard; Zest produces it, the client owns the assertion.
